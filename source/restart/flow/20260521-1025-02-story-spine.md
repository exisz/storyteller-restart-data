# 02 Story Spine — Hospital supply reversal

1. The sealed tram arrives under old Sunrise Hospital. The receiving dock recognizes the hospital seal, not the living people carrying it. Truth shift: the hospital first appears as a warehouse that consumes paper before it treats patients. Pressure: make the return shipment count as evidence before intake reclassifies it.

2. Intake sirens ask for a receiver name. Seventh Sunrise can be named as a living witness, but naming her lets the hospital search for the death record it failed to complete. Pressure: protect her name or weaponize the tower operator's contradiction.

3. The return-goods audit opens the living-paper lot. The batch refuses to print causes of death, but the clerk tries to downgrade it to defective stock. Pressure: use supply ledger and return invoice to force institutional acknowledgment.

4. The ward-zero shelf surfaces B-07/0. Linxi's original bed number is not a room; it is a receiving shelf for people whose deaths were ordered before arrival. Pressure: reverse the bed record publicly or use the missing-corpse count to pull the hospital deeper into its own ledger.

5. A patient-stock count begins. The hospital demands one body to balance the returned paper. Pressure: protect worker names and living witnesses while deciding whose legal non-death is strong enough to jam intake.

6. The living discharge counter appears. For the first time, the hospital can be made to issue paperwork that says someone may leave alive. Pressure: discharge Seventh Sunrise publicly or summon Ward Zero to confront the source of B-07/0.
